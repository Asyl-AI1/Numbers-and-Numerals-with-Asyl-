Cardinal Numbers: How to Count

These numbers are for counting things. You use them to answer "How many?".

  • Numbers 1 to 10: These are all single words (one, two, three...). For example, I have two volleyballs. üèê
  • Numbers 11 to 19: These are also single words that end in "-teen" or are special cases (eleven, twelve, fifteen...). For example, Adele has won fifteen Grammy Awards.
  • Multiples of 10: These end in "-ty" (twenty, thirty, forty...). For example, our class has twenty students.
  • Compound Numbers: For numbers from 21 to 99, you connect the tens and ones with a hyphen (e.g., twenty-one, fifty-seven). For example, there are twenty-three students in our class.
  • Large Numbers: For hundreds, thousands, and millions, you connect the number with the word (e.g., one hundred, five thousand). For example, it will cost a few thousand dollars to visit Italy.

Ordinal Numbers: How to Rank

These numbers show order or position. You use them to answer "What is the order?".

  • Rule: The general rule is to add "-th" to the end of the cardinal number (e.g., fourth, fifth, sixth).
  • Special Cases: The first three are special! (first, second, third). For example, you are in first place! üèÜ
  • Tricky Ones: The numbers that end with "1", "2", or "3" are also special, but only if the tens digit is not "1".
